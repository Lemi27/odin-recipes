Broiled Filet Mignon Recipe

Description

Broiled filet mignon is an elegant and tender steak dish that exudes sophistication and flavor. Known for its buttery texture and melt-in-your-mouth quality, this cut of beef is celebrated for its natural richness and delicate taste. When broiled, the filet develops a beautifully caramelized exterior while maintaining its juicy, tender interior. Perfectly cooked filet mignon showcases a fine balance of a savory crust and a succulent, medium-rare center that highlights the premium nature of the meat. Itâ€™s a versatile dish that can be paired with a variety of sides, elevating any meal into a gourmet experience.

Ingredients

  • cooking spray
  • 2 tablespoons packed light brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons prepared horseradish
  • 1 teaspoon grated shallot
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 4 (4 ounce) filet mignon steaks

Steps

  1. Set an oven rack about 3 inches from the heat source and preheat the oven's broiler. Lightly spray a broiler pan with cooking spray.
  2. Combine brown sugar, horseradish, shallot, and pepper in a small bowl.
  3. Place steaks on the prepared broiler pan and spread 1/2 of the brown sugar mixture over top.
  4. Broil in the preheated oven for 5 minutes. Turn steaks, spread remaining brown sugar mixture over top, and broil to desired degree of doneness, 5 to 9 minutes longer.
